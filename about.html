Who am I?

Hi, I’m Jenny! I’m a teacher living in Northern Virginia with my husband, our two kiddos, my in-laws, and a parade of fuzzy creatures. While I've bounced around the east coast my entire life, my roots stretch deep into New England and Southern Appalachia. These locations are where canning is more than just a kitchen task: it's a way of life! My grandmother taught me how to can vegetables to get us through the winter, and a close family friend from South Appalachia showed me the care and intention that goes into preserving food. Their lessons still shape how I work in the kitchen today and how I understand the stories that surround the foods we love.

I’ll be honest: I don’t always eat what I make. I have sensory issues with a lot of foods (one of my many quirks). But I love the process! I love the rhythm of it, the purpose, and the joy of giving something homemade to someone I care about. My shelves are full, and so are the hands of my family, neighbors, and coworkers.

I started Lemon and Lore because I love being in my garden, preserving what I grow, and sharing that process with others. If you’re new to canning, I hope this site helps you feel brave enough to start. You don’t need fancy tools or a perfect setup. You just need a little guidance and the willingness to try. For me, canning is about connection: to the land, to the past, and to each other. I’m so glad you’re here!

The Principles Behind Lemon and Lore

At Lemon and Lore, canning is not just a method; it's a memory, a mindset, and a way of moving through the seasons with intention. Every jar holds a story of the hands that grew the fruit, the family recipes passed down, and the quiet satisfaction of preserving something good. I want to open the process to anyone who is curious because, despite how it seems, canning doesn't have to be complicated, expensive, or intimidating. My approach is simple, rooted in tradition, and flexible. My goal is make room for real life while also making time to make preserving a part of life. Here's what guides me in the kitchen and in the stories I share:

  • Preserve with Purpose

    Canning is a way to slow down, savor, and take thoughtful care of what we've been given by using it wisely and with respect. Don't just think about the food, but focus on care and creativity with the process.

  • Start with What You Have

    You don’t need a perfect setup or a pantry full of gear. Use the tools you already own and the produce that’s available to you, then you can build from there.

  • Use What's in Season

    Seasonal produce is not only more affordable, it's often more flavorful. Whether it’s tomatoes in August or apples in October, let the calendar guide your jars.

  • Honor the Story

    Every recipe has roots. Appreciate the history and personal moments that make each preserve meaningful.

  • Share What You Make

    Canning is about connection. Giving homemade preserves to neighbors, coworkers, and family is a way to share not just food, but care and effort for those who are important in our lives.

  • Safety First, Always

    Follow tested recipes and safe canning practices! No shortcuts! Preserving food is powerful, but it has to be done right.

  • Teach with Clarity

    Whether you're new to canning or seasoned with steam burns and sticky counters, I aim to make the process approachable, safe, and satisfying.

  • Progress Over Perfection

    Not every jar will be pretty. Not every batch will be flawless. That’s okay. Every time you can, you learn something. Don't give up. Learn. Review. Reflect. Try again.

  • Celebrate the Simple

    There’s beauty in the basics. A well-sealed jar, a handwritten label, a shelf lined with color...these are small joys worth honoring and worth celebrating!
