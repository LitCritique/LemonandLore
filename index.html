Ask Jenny

Q: "I'm brand new to canning--what's the easiest thing to start with?"
-Lilly, Virginia

A: Hi Lilly! Great question—and welcome to the world of canning! I always recommend starting with water bath canning and something simple, such as strawberry jam or dilly green beans. These recipes are so forgiving, they don't require special equipment beyond a big pot and some jars, and they give you that satisfying pop when the lids seal.

Start small, label everything, and don't worry if it's not perfect—every jar is a little lesson. You'll be hooked in no time!

Canning Myth of the Month

Myth: "You can just flip the jar upside down to seal it."

Truth: This old trick might have worked for Grandma's jelly, but it's not considered safe today. Flipping jars doesn't guarantee a proper vacuum seal and can lead to spoilage or contamination. For safe, long-term storage, always use a tested water bath or pressure canning method. Let your jars cool upright and undisturbed.

Why Canning Makes Sense Right Now

Let’s face it: grocery bills aren’t what they used to be. With tariffs in2025 hitting their highest levels since the1930s, families are feeling the squeeze on their wallets. According to Yale's Budget Lab, the average household is losing about $2,500 in purchasing power this year alone. And while food inflation has cooled off a bit since2022, prices are still climbing, especially for staples like eggs and beef.

This is where canning and preserving comes in.

Canning and preserving food at home isn’t just a throwback skill anymore! Now, it’s a practical way to stretch the grocery budget, reduce waste, and take back a little control in a time when prices feel anything but stable. With ongoing supply chain hiccups and tariff-related uncertainty, having a stocked pantry of home-preserved goods can bring real peace of mind. According to the USDA and Mississippi State University Extension, home canning helps preserve the nutritional value of fresh produce and gives families a reliable food source right at home. And, when it's done safely, it's a long-term storage solution that protects against spoilage and contamination.

So, whether you're harvesting from your backyard garden, shopping at the farmer's market, or bying in bulk at the store, canning is a smart way to make your food dollars stretch just a little further.
